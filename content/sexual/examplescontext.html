Context and Impact Matter FOR ALLEN DEVELOPMENT
As you can see, there is a lot to consider while interacting with your employees and colleagues. The context, time, place and circumstances may affect whether your conduct towards another employee has crossed the line.

For example:
  • Hugging a bereaved employee at a difficult moment may be acceptable, while asking an employee for a hug would not
  • Offering an occasional compliment to someone on a new haircut or new outfit may be appropriate, while remarking about someone's appearance on a consistent basis would not


In all of your workplace interactions, it's important to remember that it's not your intent that matters; it's the impact on your colleagues and employees that you manage that determines the appropriateness of your conduct.
