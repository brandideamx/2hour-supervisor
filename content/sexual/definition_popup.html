Definition of Sexual Harassment:

Sexual harassment is a form of discrimination

Sexual harassment occurs when one person makes unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors or other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature to another person in the workplace.

What types of sexual harassment may be prohibited?

  • Sexual harassment by members of the opposite sex
  • Same-sex sexual harassment
  • Sexual harassment between members of the same or different sexual orientation or perceived sexual orientation
  • Sexual harassment between members of the same or different gender, gender identity or gender expression

Unlawful sexual harassment is prohibited by:

  • Supervisors
  • Employees/Co-Workers
  • Contingent Workers
  • Customers/Clients
  • Visitors
  • Vendors
  • Other Third Parties

Examples of harassment:

  • Unwanted touching (kissing, hugging, etc.)
  • Unwelcome sexual advances, including pressure for dates
  • Solicitation of sexual favors or other sexually related behavior by promise of rewards
  • Coercion of sexual activity by threat or punishment
  • Sexual assault
  • Discriminatory comments, remarks or slurs
  • Sexist or stereotypical remarks about men or women

More subtle examples of harassment:

  • Leering or ogling
  • Off-handed discriminatory comments, remarks, or slurs
  • Inappropriate emails, IM's, text messages, social media posts and chats whether or not intended to be humorous
  • Inappropriate cartoons, magazines, photos or other visual materials
  • Sexual innuendos in guise of humor or that create an uncomfortable atmosphere
  • Sexual jokes, remarks or questions
  • Sexual remarks about a person's body, clothing or appearance
  • Certain gender-based terms of endearment
  • Conversations about people's sex lives which are overheard by others
