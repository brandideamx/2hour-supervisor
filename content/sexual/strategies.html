Strategies for Creating an Upstander Culture FOR ALLEN DEVELOPEMENT
You can act as an upstander in large and small ways everyday. Click the green arrows to reveal examples of upstander behaviors.

Recognize and Interrupt

You can recognize offensive conduct and interrupt it. For example, tell a colleague the term they used to describe someone is offensive.

Publicly Support

You can publicly support someone who has been offended or neglected.
For example, call attention to the efforts of a colleague who has been overlooked in a meeting or group email.

Use Body Language

You can use body language to show disapproval.
For example, turn away from someone telling an offensive joke.

Comfort

You can help comfort hurt feelings.
For example, approach someone you know has been offended and offer support.
