Sexual Harassment and Technology/Social Media
Many employees are engaging with each other through cell phones and/or social media before, during and after work hours. Employers have a duty to take steps to prevent and stop workplace harassment they become aware of, even when the harassment is conducted through the use of technology/social media.

Examples:
  • Friending a co-worker on Facebook and then sending offensive messages or repeated requests for a date
  • Sending harassing, intimidating or inappropriate text messages to a subordinate
  • Posting sexually explicit or offensive photos or videos of yourself or other employees on Instagram
