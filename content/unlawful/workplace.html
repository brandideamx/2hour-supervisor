Harassment in the Workplace

Harassment is a form of discrimination and occurs when an employee is the target of unwelcome conduct, based on a legally protected characteristic.

Harassment is unlawful and will not be tolerated at your organization. However, it's important to understand that the standard for what is considered harassment may differ depending on the state in which you work.

California

  • In California, harassment is defined as a form of discrimination that occurs when an employee is the target of unwelcome conduct, based on a legally protected characteristic, that is so severe or pervasive it creates a hostile work environment, or otherwise interferes with a person's emotional tranquility or sense of well-being in the workplace.
  • Severe = a comment or act that is outrageous or offensive
  • Pervasive = conduct that occurs repeatedly

Connecticut

  • In Connecticut, It is considered harassment when a supervisor or an employee subjects another employee to unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic that is so severe or pervasive that a reasonable person would find the work environment to be intimidating, hostile or offensive ("hostile work environment").
  • Severe = one comment or act that is outrageous or offensive
  • Pervasive = conduct that occurs repeatedly
  • Additionally, sexual harassment may occur when a supervisor makes an employment decision (e.g. termination or promotion) based upon a subordinate's rejection or submission to unwelcome sexual demands. This is referred to as "quid pro quo" sexual harassment.

Delaware

  • In Delaware, harassment is considered a form of discrimination and occurs when an employee is the target of unwelcome conduct, based on a legally protected characteristic, that is so severe or pervasive it creates a hostile work environment.
  • Severe = one comment or act that is outrageous or offensive
  • Pervasive = conduct that occurs repeatedly

Illinois

  • In Illinois, harassment means any unwelcome conduct on the basis of an individual's actual or perceived race, color, religion, national origin, ancestry, age, sex, marital status, order of protection status, disability, military status, sexual orientation/gender identity, pregnancy, unfavorable discharge from the military service, or citizenship status, or arrest record that is sufficiently severe or pervasive to have the purpose or effect of substantially interfering with the individual's work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile or offensive working environment.
  • Severe = a comment or act that is outrageous or offensive
  • Pervasive = conduct that occurs repeatedly

Maine

  • In Maine, harassment means unwelcome advances on the basis of race or color, sex, sexual orientation, physical or mental disability, religion, age, ancestry or national origin that are sufficiently severe or pervasive to have the purpose or effect of unreasonably interfering with the individual's work performance or creating an intimidating, hostile or offensive working environment.
  • Severe = a comment or act that is outrageous or offensive
  • Pervasive = conduct that occurs repeatedly

New York

  • Under New York State law, harassment occurs when an individual is subjected to inferior terms, conditions or privileges of employment because of the individual's membership in a legally protected category.
  • Under New York City law, harassment occurs when an employee is treated less well based on his or her membership in a legally protected category.
  • However, in both New York State and New York City, petty slights or trivial inconveniences do not rise to the level of unlawful workplace harassment.

Other

  • Many states consider harassment to be an unlawful form of discrimination when it is based on a legally protected characteristic and that is so severe or pervasive that it creates a hostile work environment.
  • Severe = a comment or act that is outrageous or offensive
  • Pervasive = conduct that occurs repeatedly

You must review the standards for every state in which you work and/or supervise employees.
