What are Legally Protected Characteristics?

Federal:

  • Age (40 and older)
  • Citizenship
  • Color
  • Disability
  • Gender Identity
  • Genetic Information
  • National Origin
  • Pregnancy
  • Race
  • Religion
  • Sex
  • Sexual Orientation
  • Veteran/Military Status

California

In addition to those characteristics protected under Federal law, the following characteristics are protected under State law:
  • Ancestry
  • Creed
  • Disability (mental and/or physical)
  • Gender identity/expression
  • Marital status
  • Medical condition
  • Race, color
  • Sex, gender (including pregnancy, childbirth, breastfeeding, or related medical conditions)
  • Status as a victim of domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking

Connecticut

In addition to those characteristics protected under Federal law, the following characteristics are protected under State law:
  • Age
  • Ancestry
  • Gender identity or expression
  • Learning disability or physical disability, including blindness
  • Marital or civil union status
  • Intellectual disability
  • Present or past history of mental disability
  • Religious creed

Delaware

In addition to those characteristics protected under Federal law, the following characteristics are protected under State law:
  • Family responsibilities
  • Inquiring about, discussing, or disclosing wages
  • Marital status
  • Reproductive health decisions
  • Sex (including pregnancy)
  • Status as a victim of domestic violence, sexual offenses, or stalking
  • Volunteer emergency responder status

Illinois

In addition to those characteristics protected under Federal law, the following characteristics are protected under State law:
  • Age
  • Marital status
  • Ancestry
  • National origin
  • Arrest record
  • Order of protection status
  • Citizenship status
  • Pregnancy
  • Color
  • Disability
  • Unfavorable military discharge

Maine

In addition to those characteristics protected under Federal law, the following characteristics are protected under State law:
  • Age
  • Ancestry
  • Filing a claim or asserting a right under the Worker's Compensation Act
  • Gender identity/expression
  • Genetic pre-disposition
  • Physical and mental disability
  • Retaliation under the Whistleblower's Act
  • Sexual orientation or affiliation

New York State

In addition to those characteristics protected under Federal law, the following characteristics are protected under State law:
  • Caregiver status
  • Credit history
  • Partnership status
  • Sexual and other reproductive health decisions
  • Unemployment status

New York City

In addition to those characteristics protected under Federal law and New York State law, the following characteristics are protected under New York City law:
  • Age (18 and older)
  • Creed
  • Criminal history
  • Domestic violence victim status
  • Familial status
  • Gender identity/gender expression/transgender status/gender dysphoria
  • Marital status
  • Reproductive health decisions

Other State/Local

This list is not exhaustive, as legally protected characteristics may change and may not apply depending on your place of employment.
  • Ancestry
  • Genetic information
  • Atypical cellular or blood trait
  • Marital status
  • Breastfeeding
  • Religious creed
  • Domestic partnership, civil union, same sex marriage
  • Sexual orientation or affiliation
  • Unemployed status
  • Familial status
  • Victim of domestic violence
  • Gender identity/expression
  • Weight

You must review the standards for every state in which you work and/or supervise employees.
