Recognizing Common Workplace Stereotypes FOR ALLEN DEVELOPMENT
Perhaps this exercise helped you to see that you are already aware of common workplace stereotypes.

Most people strive to make business decisions based on relevant facts rather than on stereotypes. However, despite our good intentions, sometimes we are influenced by the stereotypes that we have been exposed to again and again.

Part of building self-awareness is understanding that all of us are influenced by "unconscious biases," which are often rooted in common stereotypes.

Common stereotypes include:

Physically-based stereotypes:
  • Obese people are lazy
  • Tall men are stronger, more decisive leaders
  • People with disabilities require special attention
  • Performance expectations are lower for employees with physical and mental challenges
Gender-based stereotypes:
  • Women are inferior in math and science
  • Women with children are less willing to travel for assignments
  • Men like to dominate a discussion
Age-based stereotypes:
  • Older employees are not as technologically savvy
  • Young employees have entitlement attitudes
