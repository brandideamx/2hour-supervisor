Sex Discrimination
Sex discrimination involves treating an applicant or employee unfavorably because of that person's sex. Unlike sexual harassment situations, sex discrimination involves a negative (or adverse) employment action taken against an individual based on the individual's gender.
Examples of Sex Discrimination
  • Not promoting due to pregnancy
  • Treating women differently for fear of being the target of a sexual harassment claim
  • Denying an applicant a position because of their transgender status.
  • Refusing to allow a transgender person to use the bathroom of their choice
  • Moving an employee to a different department because their co-workers are uncomfortable with their sexual orientation
  • Telling a male applicant that the position he is applying for is better suited for a woman
  • Praising a man for his aggressiveness and critiquing a woman for having the same quality
  • Allowing a client to dictate which employees they'll work with based on their gender
