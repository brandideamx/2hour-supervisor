Remedies and Reporting

Depending on the state an employee works in, a victim of harassment or discrimination may have different remedies and reporting vehicles available to them.


California

An employee or job applicant claiming to be harassed may file a complaint of discrimination with California's Department of Fair Employment and Housing (DFEH) within one year of the harassment. Employees can also pursue the matter through a private lawsuit in civil court after a complaint has been filed with DFEH and Right-to-Sue Notice has been issued.

Remedies may include:

  • Damages for emotional distress from each employer or person in violation of the law
  • Hiring or reinstatement
  • Back pay or promotion
  • Changes in the policies or practices of the employer

Connecticut

An employee or job applicant claiming to be harassed may file a complaint of discrimination with the Connecticut Commission of Human Rights and Opportunities ("CHRO") within 180 days of harassment. Employees can also pursue the matter through a private lawsuit in civil court after a complaint has been filed with CHRO and Release of Jurisdiction has been issued.

Remedies may include:

  • Back pay
  • Front pay
  • Attorney's fees and costs
  • Reinstatement
  • Cease and desist orders
  • Hiring
  • Promotion
  • Compensatory damages

Delaware

In addition to the internal reporting discussed in previous slides, an employee or job applicant in the State of Delaware may file a complaint of discrimination with Delaware Department of Labor Office of Anti-Discrimination ("DDOLOAD"). The DDOLOAD may be contacted as follows to learn how to file a complaint or report discrimination:
Phone: 302-424-1134 or 302-761-8200

Visit https://dia.delawareworks .com/discrimination

The DDOLOAD can investigate or mediate the complaint.

Remedies may include:

  • Cease and desist orders or modifying existing employment policies
  • Fines of $1,000 - $5,000 per violation for violations of the retaliation provision
  • Compensatory damages, including but not limited to general and special damages
  • Punitive damages
  • Litigation costs and attorney's fees
  • Hiring, reinstatement or promotion

Illinois

Employees have the right to contact the Illinois Department of Human Rights (IDHR) or the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC) about filing a charge of discrimination. A charge must be filed within 300 days of the alleged discriminatory action. After IDHR has completed its investigation and issued its determination, the employee may opt to proceed through the Illinois Human Rights Commission (IHRC) or by commencing a civil action in the appropriate circuit court. Where the employing entity has an effective harassment policy in place and the complaining employee fails to take advantage of that policy and allow the employer an opportunity to address the problem, such an employee may, in certain cases, lose the right to further pursue the claim against the employer.


Remedies may include:

  • Back pay
  • Lost benefits
  • Clearing of personnel file
  • Emotional damages
  • Hiring
  • Promotion
  • Reinstatement
  • Front pay where reinstatement is not possible
  • Attorney's fees and costs

New York State

An employee or job applicant in the State of New York may file a complaint of discrimination with the New York State Division of Human Rights (NYDHR) within one year of the most recent act of alleged discrimination. The NYDHR can be contacted as follows:

Phone: 1-888-392-3644

New York City

An employee or job applicant in New York City claiming to be harassed may file a complaint of discrimination with the New York City Commission on Human Rights (NYCCHR) within three years of any alleged gender-based harassment and within one year of any other alleged harassment.The NYCCHR can be contacted as follows:

Phone: 718-722-3131 or call 311 and ask for "human rights"
Web: www.nyc.gov/humanrights
Mail: 40 Rector Street, 10th Floor, New York, NY 10006

Complaints should be filed with the Law Enforcement Bureau of the NYCCHR.

Employees cannot file with both the NYCCHR and NYDHR.

Other Forums for Reporting in New York

Instead of filing a complaint with the NYDHR, employees may alternatively decide to file directly in a state court within three years of the alleged discrimination. Employees must make a choice and cannot file simultaneously in the NYDHR and court.

An employee can also file a complaint with the EEOC within 300 days of the alleged discrimination. A complaint must be filed with the EEOC before filing in federal court. For more information contact www.eeoc.gov, 1-800-669-4000, info@eeoc.gov.

Further, harassment may constitute a crime if it involves conduct like physical touching, coerced physical confinement or coerced sex acts. Employees have the option of contacting the police department in these circumstances.

Other

    In addition to the internal reporting channels outlined in the employers policy, an employee can also file a complaint with the appropriate state department or they may file a complaint with the EEOC within 300 days of the alleged discrimination. A complaint must be filed with the EEOC before filing in federal court. For more information contact www.eeoc.gov, 1-800-669-4000, info@eeoc.gov.
